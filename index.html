Operating Systems (OS) are the backbone of modern computing, serving as the intermediary between computer hardware and software applications. They provide the environment and platform for applications to run on, and handle various system resources such as memory, storage, and processor usage. There are a variety of OS platforms available, each with its own set of features and capabilities, and each is used in different contexts depending on the type of device or system it is installed on.

Mobile platforms provide the operating system and development environment for mobile applications. The two dominant mobile platforms are Android and iOS. Android is an open-source operating system developed by Google and is used by a variety of device manufacturers. iOS is the proprietary operating system developed by Apple and is used exclusively on Apple devices.
